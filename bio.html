The Basics On Where I've Been...And Where I'm Going


Hello. I am assuming that some of you visiting this website may not know very much about me, if at all. So, let me tell you a little about myself.


I am from a small-town in Vermont - yes, that is a state! I am amazed by the amount of people that do not know that. While I truely enjoyed growing up in the country, where the air smells of pine trees and the roads are far-less traveled, I could not wait to get out and experience the world.


After graduating from high school, I spent a year atKeene State Collegein Keene, New Hampshire. I found my self in another lame ol' little country town. There's only two cool things about Keene: It is where Jumanji was filmed, and it is the host of the annual pumpkinfest, which may not sound like a very big deal to all my city-friends, but for all us back-woodsers it was a big deal. I had spent the majority of my life thinking that I wanted to work with children - either as a Teacher or a Family Divorce Attorney. There are two things I was always passionate about - children, and my parents divorce (I wanted to be able to help other children get through this unfortunate tragedy). I worked at a preschool center for awhile and ultimately decided that while I do love children, maybe this was not the career path for me.


So from there I took some time to really reflect on what I am good at, what I would truly enjoy doing for the rest of my life. I was only certain about one thing – I love projects. Projects of any kind. I enjoy the research, the planning, the implementing – I thrive off the rush I get in preparing to present something I have worked so hard on. So, Public Relations seemed like a good fit for me! Considering the prestigious public relations program at theUniversity of Florida,I ventured away from my country roots and headed for the sunshine state!


However, writing has never been my strong point, which poses a challenge for me in pursuing a career in public relations. It is something that I continue to work on every day, by reading more, blogging and even by producing this website. As I continue on my journey toward a career in PR I can only hope to continue to grow and learn.
